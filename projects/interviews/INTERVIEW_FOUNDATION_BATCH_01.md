# INTERVIEW_FOUNDATION_BATCH_01

Status: prepared interview for foundational human / performance / method claims.

This is not an audit, spec, verifier, prototype plan, or assets/source_needs audit.

Purpose: clarify foundational claims before auditing style, sources, assets, modules, or Max for Live architecture.

## Questions

1. What do you already do well as an acoustic improviser?
Give concrete examples: gesture, timing, listening, breath, tension, silence, density, timbre, space, audience/body energy, long-form development.

2. What must the future electronic system never take away from your live agency?
Examples: timing, breath, instability, risk, silence, physical effort, density, form, ability to stop or redirect.

3. What does intuitive electronic creation mean for you while playing live?
Examples: few controls, right controls at the right time, physical gestures, no menu-diving, no technical distraction, clear emergency exits.

4. Which of your existing strengths should the system amplify?
Examples: drone control, breath pressure, micro-timing, timbral nuance, form intuition, tension management, physical presence.

5. What kinds of cognitive load are dangerous during performance?
Examples: too many controls, unclear labels, wrong controls at the wrong time, fear of breaking the set, latency anxiety, decision fatigue, loss of listening.

6. What would make an interface musically ergonomic for you?
Answer with situations: beginning a scene, holding pressure, increasing density, avoiding cliché, recovering from overload, returning to body, exiting freeze/drone, overriding the system.

7. What would be a caricature of your project?
Examples: techno cliché, tribal cliché, ambient wash, drone cliché, ethnic decoration, obvious drop, generic dark ritual mood, sound-design demo, overly intellectual patch.

8. What would be a successful translation from acoustic sensitivity to electronic control?
Examples: didgeridoo pressure influencing sub without replacing it, jaw harp micro-pulses influencing rhythmic granularity, breath noise opening space, silence reducing generated density.

9. What is the difference between a useful micro-test and a dangerous premature prototype?
Define acceptable micro-tests.

10. The historical repo says the next step is assets/source_needs audit. Do you still feel that is right after this method restart?
Options: yes after foundational interview; no first style/acquis audit; no first performance agency audit; no first micro-test criteria; unsure.
