# 08_AUTOMATION_VS_AUGMENTATION

## Principle

Do not automate by default.

Automation is only appropriate when the task is:

- repeatable;
- verifiable;
- reversible or low-risk;
- low-dependence on taste;
- tolerant of imperfect output;
- worth the maintenance cost.

## Augmentation

Augmentation means the AI helps the human work better while the human keeps judgment.

Use augmentation when the task involves:

- taste;
- interpretation;
- artistic direction;
- high uncertainty;
- high cost of error;
- contextual judgment;
- embodied or domain-specific expertise.

## Automation

Automation means the system performs the task with minimal human intervention.

Use automation only when:

- success criteria are clear;
- failure is detectable;
- the output can be checked;
- the action is reversible or low-risk;
- the process is stable.

## Taste test

Ask:

Does this task require taste, judgment, timing, embodied knowledge, artistic sensitivity, or strategic choice?

If yes, do not fully automate. Augment.

## 80 percent test

Ask:

If the output were only 80 percent as good as a careful human version, would it still be acceptable?

If no, do not fully automate.

## Cost of error

Classify cost of error:

- low: can be discarded;
- medium: wastes time or creates confusion;
- high: damages project direction, reputation, data, performance, safety, or artistic integrity.

High-cost tasks require human validation.

## Operational debt

Every automation creates operational debt.

Before automating, define:

- owner;
- failure mode;
- monitoring;
- rollback;
- review cadence;
- stop condition.
